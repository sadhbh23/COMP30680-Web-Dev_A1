My Journey Into Computer Science

Studying Neuroscience as an Undergraduate

How I became an MSc computer science student is slightly unconventional, as I don't have a computer science or tech background. I completed my undergraduate studies in Neuroscience at Trinity College Dublin (TCD). I began my undergraduate degree in Biological, Biomedical and Biomolecular Sciences, studying all 11 natural sciences for the first two years before specialising for my final two years and thesis. While I thoroughly enjoyed studying neuroscience, I knew I didn't want to pursue a career in the field; it wasn't the right fit.

SheCodes

After finishing my final exams, I took some time to consider what I wanted. By chance, I saw an ad on Instagram for an online service called SheCodes. It is an online front-end web development academy that provides workshops for women who want to break into tech. What I liked about SheCodes compared to other online institutions providing courses in similar topics was that they offered their services at a significantly reduced rate, as their primary goal is to help bridge the gender gap in tech. Additionally, they significantly contribute to charitable causes that benefit women worldwide for example, providing relief to women refugees in Afghanistan.

As I wanted to expand my skill set and improve my employability, I completed the first course, which covered the fundamentals of HTML5, CSS3 and JavaScript (ES6). Upon receiving my certificate, I realised that I thoroughly enjoyed every aspect of the course: learning about coding, putting that knowledge to practice, refactoring my work, debugging etc. And so, I made the decision to work through all of the remaining courses (you can see my certificates on the Gallery page of this site).

FindAMasters: How About UCD?

As I worked through the courses, I gradually had more and more skills to put on my CV. Eventually, I completed all of SheCodes Pro! After some consideration, I decided to enhance my portfolio further. I began researching other means of expanding my computer skills. It became clear that I could try to learn all of the fundamentals by myself in a self-directed learning approach. But as someone who thrives in a structured environment, I thought I would be better off investigating higher education routes again. I came across the MSc Computer Science (Conversion) course using the FindAMasters website (which I highly recommend for anyone seeking further academic studies).

I spoke with some friends who either had a background in computer science, had attended UCD, or both. I wanted their insight on whether or not it would be worth it to apply to the course. They all concluded that it was very well structured, the content was thorough, and it would benefit me and my career progression immensely. With all that advice on board, I worked on my application for 10 days, honing it to perfection. A few weeks passed, and eventually, I received a response from the university; it was an acceptance letter! And now, several months later, I am 5 weeks into the course. For more details on the MSc itself, please refer to the MSc Info page of this website.
