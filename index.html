This Project

For this project, I had the choice of 3 different websites I could create: a personal website, a website for a piece of media or a website for a charity or organisation of your choice. I decided on a personal website as I thought it would fit nicely in my portfolio.

As someone with a keen interest in visual design and UX/UI, I aimed to make the look and feel of the website modern, streamlined and relatively minimalistic. I emphasised the contrast between the dark and light colours to create visual interest whilst avoiding using pure white (#ffffff) or black (#000000) anywhere to reduce eye strain and fatigue. Additionally, I incorporated purples, pinks and gradients to have pops of colour that I feel reflect my personality without being too visually overwhelming.

Module: (COMP30680) Web-App Development

Assignment 1: HTML5 & CSS3

I created this personal portfolio website for my web application development module. Before this module, I had gained experience in front-end web development, having completed courses and coded a couple of projects already; however, this assignment presented a unique challenge as it necessitated using only HTML5 and CSS3, no libraries or frameworks. It has been quite some time since I created a large project in pure HTML5 and CSS3. I am quite a fan of the Bootstrap framework: I often use it in my projects. Nevertheless, I was excited to return to basics and build on the fundamentals again.

There were quite a few specifications for this project. Most importantly, it needed to include 5 pages: a homepage, a gallery page (one that displayed images in a structured format) and 3 pages of our choice. I chose to include an about page, a page with further info on my MSc and a contact page, as you will see when you look through the website!
